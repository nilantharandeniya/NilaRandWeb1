About

Institutions, AI, and the resilience gap

Nilantha is a researcher at the Global Disaster Resilience Centre, University of Huddersfield, working with Professor Richard Haigh and Professor Dilanthi Amaratunga at Loughborough University. His work centres on the Institutional Mediation Principle, developed from fieldwork across the UK, Australia, and Sri Lanka, including a Visiting Research Fellowship at Western Sydney University's Centre for Smart Modern Construction.

Alongside the research, he teaches at Manchester Metropolitan University International College, co-leads an IEEE standards working group on ethical AI in climate projections, and edits Fields, the University of Huddersfield's student research journal.

Research

Current lines of work

From institutional theory to applied climate-health modelling, the through-line is the same question: how do we build AI systems institutions can actually trust and use under disaster and climate risk.

01

Institutional AI Readiness Framework

Doctoral research explaining the adoption gap between government agencies and academic institutions on equivalent infrastructure. Currently in rebuttal at AIES 2026.

02

Temperature Extremes & Respiratory Mortality

Explainable machine learning project linking UK temperature data to weekly respiratory mortality records, with Nelum Bopitiyegedara and Lahiru Kodithuwakku.

03

Urban Vulnerability, Sri Lanka

Two papers in development on AI-based urban morphological assessment, covering Colombo, Kurunegala, and Galle.

04

Flood-Driven Dengue Dynamics

An empirical study of lag relationships between flood events and dengue transmission in Sri Lanka, with NBRO, the Disaster Management Centre, and the MOH Epidemiology Unit.
