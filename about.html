About Nilantha

Researcher and educator working at the intersection of institutional theory, artificial intelligence, and disaster resilience.

Nilantha Randeniya (Jude N. Randeniya) is a researcher at the Global Disaster Resilience Centre, University of Huddersfield, working with Professor Richard Haigh and Professor Dilanthi Amaratunga (Loughborough University). His research centres on the Institutional Mediation Principle: an explanation for why government agencies adopt artificial intelligence far more slowly and unevenly than academic institutions, despite having comparable infrastructure.

The work draws on 45 semi-structured interviews, five focus groups, and over 100 policy documents collected across Australia, the UK, and Sri Lanka, including fieldwork carried out during a Visiting Research Fellowship at the Centre for Smart Modern Construction, Western Sydney University, under Professor Srinath Perera. The framework was validated by 53 international experts at a Royal Academy of Engineering Frontiers Symposium in Tokyo.

Before starting his PhD, Nilantha worked across urban planning and project coordination roles in Sri Lanka, including as a Town Planner for the Federation of Sri Lankan Local Government Authorities, a Project Coordinator at John Keells Holdings, and an Assistant Lecturer and Research Assistant at the University of Sri Jayewardenepura. He holds a BSc in Urban Planning from the University of Moratuwa and a BSc in Information Technology from the University of Colombo - a combination that shows up directly in his current work bridging spatial planning, policy, and applied AI.

Alongside the PhD, Nilantha teaches at Manchester Metropolitan University International College, co-leads IEEE P7021 - a working group setting ethical standards for AI in climate projections - and edits Fields, the University of Huddersfield's postgraduate research journal.
