Early Life

A small village, a family that never stopped trying, and the years that shaped why this work matters.

I was born in Olanda village, Hendala, Wallata, a very small village. My parents, Benedict Randeniya and Dammika Priyanthi Randeniya, raised six of us: four sisters, Amila Dilrukshi, Achala Lakmali, Aruni Thushari, and Ajantha Kumari, and one brother, Akila Prasad. We lived in a small house with one main hall room and one kitchen. My father worked as a postman. My mother was a housewife.

In 2003, my father had an incident at work and was temporarily let go. It took until 2010 for things to settle. He had six children and a wife to look after, and he did everything he could to put food on a table that had to stretch a long way. Every year, monsoon floods hit our area. Some years we lost our food, and our floor became our bed when the water came in. That experience is one of the reasons I do the work I do now.

I went to Nayakakanda Boys' School, which became Nayakakanda Archbishop's College around 2015. I stayed at that one school for my entire school life. I failed my Grade 5 Scholarship exam, scoring 43 out of 200. I wasn't a keen student at the start. I passed my O/Levels, but my parents couldn't afford to send me to a well-known school in the city, so I stayed where I was.

In 2014, my school started an Advanced Level Arts stream for the first time. I didn't know what direction to take, until three teachers changed that for me: Mr. Prabash Kirthi, now Dr. Keerthi Prabash, Mr. Prasad, and Mr. Sesath. My principal, Mrs. Roshini, arranged extra classes in the city for me and helped with the cost. I passed with three A's, and in 2017 I was accepted to the University of Moratuwa to study a BSc (Hons) in Town and Country Planning.

Early in my degree, my father fell ill and I had to leave university and miss my first semester. I came back, worked hard, and graduated in 2023 with First Class Honours, ranked in the top 2% of my class.
