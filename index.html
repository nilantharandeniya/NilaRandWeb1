About

Institutions, AI, and the resilience gap

Jude is a final-year PhD candidate at the Global Disaster Resilience Centre, University of Huddersfield, supervised by Professor Richard Haigh and Professor Dilanthi Amaratunga (Loughborough University). His research centres on the Institutional Mediation Principle: an explanation for why government agencies adopt artificial intelligence far more slowly and unevenly than academic institutions, despite having comparable infrastructure.

The work draws on 45 semi-structured interviews, five focus groups, and over 100 policy documents collected across Australia, the UK, and Sri Lanka — including fieldwork carried out under a Turing Scheme placement at Western Sydney University. The framework was validated by 53 international experts at a Royal Academy of Engineering Frontiers Symposium in Tokyo.

Alongside the PhD, Jude is Subject Leader for Analytical Techniques and Introduction to Programming at Manchester Metropolitan University International College, Co-lead and Secretary of IEEE P7021 on ethical AI in climate projections, and Editor of The Field Journal at the University of Huddersfield.

Research

Current lines of work

From institutional theory to applied climate-health modelling, the through-line is the same question: how do we build AI systems that institutions can actually trust and use under disaster and climate risk.

01

Institutional AI Readiness Framework

Doctoral research explaining the adoption gap between government agencies (scoring 2.1/5) and academic institutions (4.0/5) on equivalent infrastructure. Currently in rebuttal at AIES 2026.

02

AI4CASCADE

Artificial Intelligence for Compound Climate and Health Cascade Predictions — a NERC IRF application hosted at Loughborough, modelling compound drought–flood–health cascades across the UK and Sri Lanka.

03

Temperature Extremes & Respiratory Mortality

Joint explainable ML project with Nelum Bopitiyegedara and Lahiru Kodithuwakku, linking HadUK-Grid temperature data to ONS weekly respiratory mortality records. Now in feature engineering.

04

Urban Vulnerability, Sri Lanka

Two papers in development: an urban morphological assessment for the International Journal of Disaster Resilience in the Built Environment, and a polycentric vulnerability study of Colombo for Cities (Elsevier).
